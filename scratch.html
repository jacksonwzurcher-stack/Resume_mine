    • Why do I do what I do?

    For me, backpacking is the ultimate expression of the dichotomy of control; when you strip away the noise of modern life and carry only what is essential, you are left with nothing but the raw terrain and your own resolve. I gravitate toward a minimalist approach because it clears the clutter from my mind, forcing me to focus on the terrain, the movement, and the clarity that comes from self-sufficiency. By embracing a lighter pack, I can move with more intention and agility through the mountains, treating each trip as a practice in precision where every piece of gear has a specific, necessary purpose. It isn’t just about covering miles; it is about the discipline of simplifying until all that remains is the connection between the trail and the stillness of the wilderness.
